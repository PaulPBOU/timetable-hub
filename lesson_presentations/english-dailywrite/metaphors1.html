Metaphors

Figurative Language in Action

What is a Metaphor?

When you use a metaphor, you are saying that a person, place, animal, or thing IS something else (not just like it).

It compares two things, but the comparison is not literal. It's a way to paint a strong picture in the reader's mind.

"My brother is the biggest piglet I know."

Examples of Metaphors

He is a shining star.

Life is a roller coaster.

The world is a stage.

Her hair is a golden river.

Wait... what about Similes?

Simile

Compares two things using the words 'like' or 'as'.

"The car moved like a snail."

Metaphor

Compares two things by saying one thing IS the other.

"The house was a zoo."
